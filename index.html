You have to know what's in the box before you can think outside of it.

I am familiar with various full-stack languages, libraries, and frameworks, but my true love lies with front-end. Like editing a manuscript, the work could go on forever, no creative project is ever perfect, but there's something magical about the moment you resign from the keyboard and call it complete.

For the better part of my life, I told myself and everyone else I wasn’t creative. I remember the shift, in middle school, when image became a concern. Art class became a source of anxiety, a place where my 'best' looked completely different...and amateur. Instead, I focused on measurable successes.

How did I get out of this loop of self-deprecation? Bodybuilding. I spent almost three years training to enter the women's IFBB physique division, a little too intense apparently, which left me with trauma induced carpel tunnel. Yoga and ice packs are my best friends now, but I learned a lot from all that time with the weights. Your reality is a product of your creation. I stopped waiting to see where life took me. I started thinking about where I wanted to go.

I write fiction under the handle WB Welch. I am an Apple devotee. I've always had an affinity for leather journals and colored ink pens. I think eating organic and NON-GMO is important, so is eating your vegetables. I think science is cool, I enjoy photography, I love scary movies, I will know how to do an ollie before I'm thirty-five, and I am a web developer.
